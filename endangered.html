Endangered Species

 

Introduction

When you hear the word “teenager”, do you think of a down-to-earth, realistic generation who knows and understands what is happening in the world around them? When you see a teenager walking down the street, what is he doing? Is he gazing at the world around him, admiring its beauty? Or is he staring at his phone, with earphones in and mouth half open?

Unfortunately, the latter is becoming increasingly popular, with technology reaching new heights every day. People’s go-to activity when they are bored or need a rest is lounging on their bed, pulling out their phone and scrolling through instagram or tik tok. And they will do nothing else for the rest of the day.

Why is this the case?

 

Many children are introduced to technology from a young age. Whether it be a TV or a phone, the technology sticks to them. This is the same for books, you are more likely to enjoy books when you are older if you have grown up with them than not liking them. Technology is a big part of society these days. If you don’t have a smartphone, it would seem you can’t be contacted, you have nothing to do the entire day, you can’t schedule anything, your whole life revolves around your phone. And while a phone is a big part of our day-to-day life, it’s not the only thing that has entertainment value.

I’m talking about books. Books are a place to tell stories, get lost in your imagination, a great place to get lost in your own little world, and while they don’t have an OLED display, they are still great for entertainment!
