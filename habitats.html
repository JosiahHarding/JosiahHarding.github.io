Habitats in Danger

 

Book loving communities

Due to the increase in technology, there is a decrease in book sales, making it harder and harder for bookstores to stay open. Because if no one will buy their stuff, then they won’t have enough money to stay open. This will limit the availability to buy books and make it harder for book-lovers to enjoy reading. Of course there are libraries but if people won’t borrow books, then those libraries will close. Governments will realise that libraries aren’t valuable to people anymore and will use the area for other purposes.

In time, books will cease to exist. Online learning has already been put into place, diminishing the need for workbooks and textbooks, soon everything will be digital, and books will become extinct.

What is the problem with this?

 

Many book-loving people find great joy in turning a page, feeling the texture of it as you flip pages. People will lose that feeling, and instead of holding a book in their hands, they’ll be holding a cold metal light-up brick with voice command. For some people, phones and video games just aren’t appealing to them, and they’ll find themselves with a whole lot of time on their hands and nothing to do.

This will mean that worst case scenario we will be left with a generation that is indoors, whose world revolves around a piece of hardware, with no friends except for other people online in games, who they will probably never see in real life. And friends that they will meet in person will have nothing to talk about except video games, since that’s all they’re interested about. I don’t see good in any of that.
